Ruaha National Park

Ruaha National Park, located in the heart of Tanzania, is the largest national park in the country and one of Africa's most spectacular wildlife destinations. Known for its rugged scenery, great wildlife densities, and off-the-beaten-path experiences, Ruaha is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The park is famous for its large population of elephants, lions, and diverse bird species.

What to Expect

  • Large prides of lions and one of the largest elephant populations in East Africa
  • Scenic landscapes with the Great Ruaha River, baobab trees, and rolling hills
  • Opportunities for walking safaris and game drives in a remote wilderness
  • Ideal for bird watchers with over 570 species recorded

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Ruaha National Park is during the dry season, from June to October. Wildlife is easier to spot as animals congregate around water sources, and the vegetation is less dense. However, birdwatchers may also enjoy the wet season (November to April), when migratory birds are present and the landscape is lush.
