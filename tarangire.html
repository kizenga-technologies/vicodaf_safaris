Tarangire National Park

Tarangire National Park is one of Tanzania’s most spectacular safari destinations, renowned for its ancient baobab trees, massive elephant herds, and seasonal migration of wildlife. Located slightly off the main safari route, Tarangire offers a quieter, more intimate safari experience compared to its neighboring parks.

The park is a haven for bird watchers, boasting over 550 bird species, and home to diverse wildlife including lions, leopards, cheetahs, giraffes, wildebeests, zebras, and buffaloes. The Tarangire River, a permanent water source, draws animals during the dry season, creating ideal game viewing opportunities.

Visitors can enjoy classic game drives, guided walking safaris, and cultural visits to nearby Maasai villages. Tarangire’s landscape is dotted with rolling hills, swamps, and open grasslands, all dominated by the majestic baobab trees, some believed to be over a thousand years old.

Whether you're a first-time visitor or a returning adventurer, Tarangire promises a raw and unforgettable African safari experience.
